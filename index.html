History of Treaty 1

Negotiations: The talks lasted eight days and involved over a thousand First Nations participants. While the Crown sought peaceful land annexation for settlement and agriculture, the Indigenous nations aimed to protect their traditional lands and way of life amidst the decline of the buffalo and settler influx . Written Terms: The written treaty required the nations to surrender a large territory in southern Manitoba. In exchange, the Crown promised reserves (160 acres per family of five), a one-time gratuity of $3 per person, annual annuities, schools, and a prohibition on alcohol on reserves .
